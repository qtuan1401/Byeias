Here’s a look at the life of former National Security Adviser and Secretary of State Henry Kissinger. Personal:Birth date: May 27, 1923, Birth place: Furth, Germany, Birth name: Heinz Alfred Kissinger, Father: Louis Kissinger, a teacher, Mother: Paula (Stern) Kissinger, Marriages: Nancy (Maginnes) Kissinger (March 30,  ) Ann Fleischer (  divorced) Children: with Ann Fleischer: Elizabeth, David, Education: Harvard University, B. A. 1950 M. A. 1952 Ph. D. 1954, Military: US Army,   Captain, Religion: Jewish, Other Facts:Kissinger’s name was changed to Henry when his family immigrated to the United States to escape the Nazis. Attended high school at night while working at a factory during the day. First person to serve as both national security adviser and secretary of state. Timeline:1938   The Kissinger family immigrates to the United States, settling in New York. June 19, 1943   Becomes a US citizen.      Harvard University faculty member.     Associate Director of Harvard’s Center for International Affairs.     Consultant, Weapons Systems Evaluation Group of the Joint Chiefs of Staff.     Consultant, National Security Council.     Consultant, US Arms Control and Disarmament Agency.     Consultant, US Department of State.     Assistant to the President for National Security Affairs for President Nixon. 1969   Helps initiate the Strategic Arms Limitation Talks (SALT) with the Soviet Union. 1972   Kissinger and President Nixon are named Time Magazine’s Men of the Year. September 23,   20, 1977   Secretary of State under Presidents Richard Nixon and Gerald Ford. 1973   Wins the Nobel Peace Prize, with Le Duc Tho, for negotiating the end of the Vietnam War. Le Duc Tho declines the award.     Assistant to the President for National Security Affairs for President Ford.  1982   Opens international consulting firm Kissinger Associates, Inc. 1982   Has triple coronary bypass surgery.  November 2002   Is appointed by President George W. Bush to lead the National Commission on Terrorist Attacks Upon the United States (also known as the   Commission). December 13, 2002   Resigns as chairman of the   Commission, citing controversy over possible conflicts of interest with clients of his consulting firm. March 2005   Undergoes an angioplasty procedure. May 18, 2006   Is awarded the Dwight D. Eisenhower Medal for Leadership and Service.  July 15, 2014   Undergoes heart surgery to replace an aortic valve.
