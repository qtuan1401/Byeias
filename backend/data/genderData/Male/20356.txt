(REUTERS) Gun   ballot measures in four states are expected to pass on Tuesday, opinion polls show, after gun safety advocates poured a massive amount of money into backing the initiatives. [In Maine and Nevada, residents will vote on whether to mandate universal background checks for firearm sales, including private handgun transactions.  If those two measures pass, half of all Americans would live in states that have such expanded checks. Eighteen states and Washington, D. C. have already approved similar laws.
