Gale Holland writes at the Los Angeles Times that although New York City remains the number one city for homelessness in America, the City of Angels far and away leads the nation in “unsheltered” homeless people.   The annual Los Angeles homeless count ended early Friday amid disappointment that the street encampments rankling residents across the city are still on the rise. The   event, which marshaled 7, 700 volunteers for a    hand count of homeless people and encampments, was held to update Los Angeles County’s homeless count, which stood at 47, 000 in 2016.
